STUART
SINGLETON
songwriter · producer · label founder · builder  |  nashville, tn
Son of legendary record producer Shelby Singleton — the man who bought Sun Records from Sam Phillips — Stuart grew up where music history meets music business. He started his first company on his 18th birthday, spent four years booking 300+ shows across Nashville's indie scene, then launched Cake Records in 2018 as a full-service label and publisher. Under the alias Casein, he's produced lofi instrumentals with over 6 million Apple Music plays and editorial placements across every major platform.
Today he builds the infrastructure independent artists need: software for organizing and strategizing a catalog, a creative home for a label and its people, and the tooling that lets one person run like a team. He's also working to carry his family's legacy forward in Nashville.

When he's not writing songs, shipping code, or structuring deals, he's behind a camera, with ten years of photography spanning Nashville to the south of France, or in the kitchen making something from scratch.

His operating philosophy is simple: jack of all trades, master of none, but better than a master of one. He thinks in webs, not lines. Builds things to process the world. And believes that independence doesn't mean isolation; it means owning your work while choosing who you collaborate with.
